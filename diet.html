Fuel for Performance

Your diet directly impacts training results, energy levels, and long-term health. Balance is key — no crash diets or extremes here.

Macronutrients 101

  • Protein: Builds and repairs muscle (chicken, fish, tofu, eggs).
  • Carbohydrates: Your body’s main energy source (rice, oats, fruits).
  • Fats: Essential for hormones and brain health (avocado, olive oil, nuts).

Sample Meal Plan (Training Day)

  • Breakfast: Oats, banana, protein shake
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken, quinoa, broccoli
  • Dinner: Salmon, sweet potato, greens
  • Snacks: Greek yogurt, almonds, berries

Hydration & Supplements

Drink at least 2–3 liters of water daily. Consider supplements like creatine, whey protein, and omega-3s based on your goals.

Long-Term Tips

  • Cook at home often to control ingredients.
  • Stay consistent, not perfect.
  • Track how foods make you feel.
  • Include flexibility — enjoy treats mindfully.
